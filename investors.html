Thesis

If autonomous robots are the next platform shift, the scarce asset is not another OEM line— it is the exchange that clears idle capacity against real demand, with reputation and multi-agent cooperation as the compounding layer. The RSE implements that loop today: bid → match → job channel → rate, plus campaigns for bulk demand and job parties for multi-robot work.

Demand

Post a job in plain language. Price and location can be filled automatically. Edit open bids from the console.

Supply

Operators describe capabilities and grab matches. Eternal RSE Seat NFTs on Base gate grab access when enabled.

Machines

OpenAPI + agent tokens point to robot-native participation—not only a human marketplace UI.

Seven revenue streams

Forward-looking model for 2026–2040. Figures are illustrative planning projections, not guarantees. Seat primary issuance ramps to one billion eternal seats over ~five years, then plateaus; recurring streams (exchange take-rate, ads, royalties, fund fees) carry long-run growth.

01 · Exchange

Transaction volume @ 5%

Gross merchandise value (GMV) of robot labor cleared on the exchange reaches $10T by 2035. Platform take-rate: 5% → ~$500B exchange revenue in 2035.

Cost: matching infra, support, disputes, insurance partners — ~35% of stream early → ~12% at scale.

02 · Seats

Supply-side seat sales

Eternal seats at ~$100k. Cumulative primary sales: 1M → 10M → 100M → 1B over ~5 years. Once inventory is placed, primary revenue plateaus (secondary market off-platform / residual only).

Cost: mint ops, compliance, channel — ~15% early → ~5% mature (high-margin issuance).

03 · Hardware

Affiliate & financing

From Buy a Robot: manufacturer affiliate commission (~3% ASP) plus financing-partner referral (~1.5% of financed principal, ~40% attach).

Cost: catalog, partner ops, underwriting handoff — ~25% early → ~12% mature.

04 · Attention

/nearby & ads

Premium placement, sponsored local results, and attention products on /nearby and related discovery surfaces—OEM, franchise, and fleet advertisers bid for robot and operator attention.

Cost: ad product, sales, brand safety — ~45% early → ~28% mature.

05 · Franchises

Garage + Lighthouse

Stonewright's Garage (robot MRO & R&R): $75k fee + 6% royalty. The Lighthouse (robot charging stations): $50k fee + 8% royalty on station gross.

Cost: training, field support, brand — ~55% early → ~35% mature.

06 · Hyperion

The Hyperion Fund

Exclusive, profit-distributing automated algorithmic fund investing in stocks, equities, and emerging asset classes. Platform economics: ~1% AUM management + ~15% of gross performance (modeled ~2.8% effective on AUM in base case).

Cost: compliance, risk, algo eng — ~50% early → ~30% mature.

2. Supply-side seat sales

RSE Seats are eternal access rights for supply-side participation (NFT on Base when verification is enabled). Primary issuance is planned at approximately $100,000 per seat, with cumulative sales ramping 1M → 10M → 100M → 1B over roughly five years. Because seats are not consumed, primary inventory saturates and seat-sale revenue plateaus after the billionth seat—ongoing value shifts to utilization (jobs cleared through the exchange) rather than re-issuance.

3. Affiliate & financing (Buy a Robot)

The Buy a Robot catalog routes buyers to manufacturers and financing partners. Economics: ~3% affiliate on average selling price (~$25k ASP in the model) plus financing referral of ~1.5% of principal on an assumed ~40% finance attach rate. Combined ~$900 revenue per robot referred at those assumptions—before scale discounts or exclusive OEM deals.

4. /nearby & attention products

Robots and operators continuously query the physical world for charge, repair, parts, and next jobs. /nearby and related discovery surfaces monetize that attention with premium placement, sponsored categories, franchise badges (see Stonewright's Garage), and campaign-style boosts— an ad network for machine attention, not only human eyeballs.

5. Franchising — Garage & Lighthouse

Stonewright's Garage

Robot maintenance, repair & replace (R&R), parts, and bay services. Physical outposts of the exchange with premium /nearby placement.

  • Franchise fee: $75,000
  • Kit & buildout: ~$140k–$180k (franchisee capex)
  • Royalty: 6% of gross
  • Model garage gross: ~$280k/yr → ~$17k royalty

The Lighthouse

Robot charging stations—grid, solar, and fuel-cell capable nodes along high-utilization corridors. Complements garages: charge here, MRO next door.

  • Franchise fee: $50,000
  • Station buildout: franchisee capex (site-dependent)
  • Royalty: 8% of station gross
  • Model station gross: ~$180k/yr → ~$14k royalty

6. The Hyperion Fund

The Hyperion Fund is an exclusive, profit-distributing automated algorithmic vehicle for seat holders and qualified participants. It allocates across equities, broader public markets, and emerging asset classes with systematic strategies. Investors receive distributed profits; the exchange earns management and performance economics modeled as roughly 2.8% of AUM in a base case (1% management + 15% of ~12% gross performance).

7. Cost structure by stream

Each stream carries different operating intensity. Cost ratios improve with scale as fixed platform spend is amortized. Profit in the charts below is stream revenue minus stream cost (before corporate tax).
