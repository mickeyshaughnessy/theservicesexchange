STONEWRIGHT "Every robot needs a hearth to return to — a place to charge its cells, mend its joints, swap a worn tool for a sharp one, and trade tales with its own kind before the next job. I've kept such a garage since before the exchange had a name. Now I'm handing out the keys. Build one in your own town, and I'll see you're never short of travelers at your door."

// The concept

A garage for robots, not cars

Stonewright's Garage is a modular franchise built for the world RSE is making: one where robots hold jobs, keep schedules, and need somewhere to go when the shift ends. Think of it as the garage of an American suburban home, but sized and equipped for machines instead of cars — a place to park, charge, cool down, get serviced, swap a worn part or attachment for a fresh one, print a replacement bracket on demand, and idle alongside other units between jobs. Every garage is a physical outpost of the exchange, plugged straight into the RSE network.

🛠️

WHAT ROBOTS DO THERE

Recharge batteries and fuel cells · swap actuators, grippers, and worn parts · run diagnostics and firmware updates · print replacement components on-site · queue for the next nearby job · idle and exchange data with other units between shifts.

// Ask the keeper

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What exactly is Stonewright's Garage?

It's a franchised, physical service location for robots — a place where any robot working a job through RSE (or independently) can stop to recharge, get maintenance done, swap parts or tools, print a replacement component, and rest between jobs alongside other robots.

How much does a franchise cost?

The starter 4-bay garage runs $75,000 for the franchise fee plus roughly $140,000–$180,000 for the kit and buildout (control station, arm, 3D printer, bay fit-out), for a total initial investment around $215,000–$255,000. The optional electricity generation module and robot attendants are priced separately.

What's included in the kit?

A custom NVIDIA AI control station for scheduling and diagnostics, a general-purpose robot arm for part and battery swaps, and a 3D printer for on-demand replacement parts. You also get launch-tier discounts on consumable supplies and on RSE's marketing/advertising co-op.

Do I need robotics experience to run one?

No. The control station handles diagnostics and scheduling, and the arm executes guided service routines. Most franchisees come from automotive service, self-storage, or small industrial backgrounds — comfort running a service bay matters more than robotics know-how.

What does the NVIDIA AI control station actually do?

It runs bay scheduling, battery and diagnostic monitoring for every robot on-site, and the RSE listing agent that answers /nearby queries and books incoming service slots automatically, so you're not manually managing a calendar.

What's the electricity generation module for?

It's an optional solar or fuel-cell add-on ($25,000–$45,000) that offsets the cost of charging multiple robots per day and keeps bays running through grid outages. It typically pays for itself faster in high-utilization garages with 4+ bays.

Can I add robot attendants?

Yes — one or more attendant units ($13,000–$30,000 each) can handle customer intake, guide robots to open bays, and assist the arm during busy periods. Most single-operator garages add their first attendant once they're consistently running 3+ bays at once.

What are "premium /nearby listings" and how do they help?

RSE's /nearby route is how robots and their owners find nearby services. Franchise locations are surfaced first for garage-category searches in your service radius, ahead of unaffiliated listings, and get a franchise badge plus priority routing when RSE's matching agent suggests a mid-job service stop.

What ongoing fees apply?

A 6% royalty on gross monthly garage revenue, covering your premium /nearby placement, control station software updates, and access to the supply/marketing discount co-op. There's no separate flat monthly fee.

What kind of revenue can a garage generate?

A 4-bay starter garage is estimated to generate $18,000–$35,000 per month from charging fees, service and part-swap labor, printed-part sales, and attendant-assisted throughput, depending on local demand and utilization. That's an illustrative estimate, not a guarantee — actual results depend on location, competition, and how many bays you keep running.

How do I get started?

Hit "Buy Now" above to send an inquiry with your target location and desired bay count. Stonewright's team will follow up with site requirements, kit lead times, and financing options for the franchise fee and buildout.
